1. Why Study in the USA?

The United States remains the world's #1 destination for international students, hosting over 1 million international students in 2025-26. For students from Hyderabad, the USA offers unmatched academic excellence, career opportunities, and cultural exposure.

  • Academic Excellence — Home to 8 of the world's top 10 universities (QS Rankings 2026)
  • Research & Innovation — The USA spends more on R&D than any other country, ideal for STEM students
  • OPT Work Rights — 12 months of post-study work (36 months for STEM fields) via OPT
  • Diverse Programs — 4,000+ accredited universities offering every imaginable specialization
  • Earning Potential — Average salary for MS graduates: $75,000–$120,000/year (₹60–95 Lakhs)
  • Global Network — Build connections with people from 200+ countries
  • Flexible Education System — Change majors, take interdisciplinary courses, get hands-on experience

5. Admission Requirements

For Master's Programs (MS/MBA)

  • Bachelor's Degree — Minimum 60% aggregate (3.0 GPA equivalent), higher for top universities
  • GRE Score — 300-320+ (some universities are GRE-optional in 2026)
  • GMAT Score — 600-720+ for MBA programs
  • English Proficiency — TOEFL 80-100+ / IELTS 6.5-7.5+ / Duolingo 110-130+
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) — A compelling essay explaining your academic goals
  • Letters of Recommendation (LOR) — 2-3 from professors or employers
  • Resume / CV — Academic and professional achievements
  • Work Experience — Preferred for MBA (2-5 years), optional for MS

6. F-1 Student Visa Process

The F-1 visa is the most common student visa for the USA. Here's the step-by-step process:

  1. Receive I-20 — After admission, your university issues the I-20 form
  2. Pay SEVIS Fee — $350 via fmjfee.com, receive I-901 receipt
  3. Complete DS-160 — Online non-immigrant visa application form
  4. Schedule Interview — Book appointment at US Consulate in Hyderabad
  5. Pay Visa Fee — $185 (₹15,000 approx.) MRV fee
  6. Attend Interview — 5-10 minute interview at the US Consulate, Hyderabad
  7. Receive Visa — If approved, passport returned with visa stamp in 3-5 business days

7. OPT & Post-Study Work

OPT (Optional Practical Training) is one of the biggest advantages of studying in the USA:

  • Standard OPT — 12 months of work authorization for all F-1 graduates
  • STEM OPT Extension — Additional 24 months (total 36 months) for STEM fields like CS, Engineering, Data Science
  • CPT — Curricular Practical Training allows paid internships during your studies
  • H-1B Visa — Employer-sponsored work visa after OPT (6-year duration, leads to Green Card)

Average OPT starting salaries for Indian graduates: $65,000 – $120,000/year depending on field and location.
