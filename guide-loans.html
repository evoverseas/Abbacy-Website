1. Education Loan Overview

An education loan is the most common way Indian students fund their international education. Over 70% of Indian students studying abroad use some form of education loan. The good news? Indian banks and NBFCs offer competitive rates, and the process has become much more streamlined in recent years.

  • Loan amounts — ₹4 Lakhs to ₹1.5 Crore+ depending on bank and collateral
  • Interest rates — 8% to 13% per annum (varies by bank and loan type)
  • Repayment — Starts 6-12 months after course completion (moratorium period)
  • Tenure — 5 to 15 years for repayment
  • Tax benefit — Interest paid is fully deductible under Section 80E

2. Types of Education Loans

A. Secured Loans (With Collateral)

  • Require property, fixed deposits, or other assets as security
  • Higher loan amounts — up to ₹1.5 Crore or more
  • Lower interest rates — typically 8-10%
  • Available from most banks — SBI, BOB, PNB, ICICI, Axis
  • Best for: Students needing large amounts for expensive universities

B. Unsecured Loans (Without Collateral)

  • No asset security required
  • Typically up to ₹40-50 Lakhs
  • Higher interest rates — typically 10.5-13%
  • Usually require a co-borrower with good income
  • Available from NBFCs — HDFC Credila, Avanse, InCred
  • Best for: Students without property collateral, going to top-ranked universities

C. International Lenders (No Co-signer Required)

  • No collateral, no Indian co-signer needed
  • Up to $100,000+ (₹80 Lakhs+)
  • Available only for top 250-500 ranked universities
  • Lenders: Prodigy Finance, MPOWER, Leap Finance
  • Higher rates — 11-15%
  • Best for: Students with strong profiles going to top universities

4. Eligibility Criteria

Student (Borrower)

  • Indian citizen
  • Age 18-35 years
  • Secured admission at a recognized foreign university
  • Minimum academic record: 50-60% (varies by bank)
  • No history of loan default

Co-Borrower (Usually a Parent)

  • Must be an earning member — salaried or self-employed
  • Good CIBIL score — ideally 700+ (750+ for unsecured loans)
  • Stable income to demonstrate repayment ability
  • Age typically below 60-65 at the time of loan closure

5. Documents Required

Student Documents

  1. 10th and 12th mark sheets
  2. Bachelor's degree mark sheets and certificate
  3. Admission offer letter (or conditional offer)
  4. GRE / GMAT / IELTS / TOEFL score card
  5. Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  6. Valid passport (first & last page copies)
  7. 2 passport-size photographs
  8. Cost of study letter / I-20 (for USA)
  9. Scholarship letter (if applicable)

Co-Borrower Documents

  1. PAN card and Aadhaar card
  2. Last 3 years' Income Tax Returns (ITR)
  3. Last 6 months' bank statements
  4. Salary slips (last 3 months) — for salaried
  5. Business proof and financials — for self-employed
  6. Property documents (if secured loan)
  7. CIBIL report authorization

6. Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. Get Admission Offer — Secure your university admission (conditional or unconditional)
  2. Compare Lenders — Research 3-4 banks/NBFCs, compare rates and terms
  3. Check Eligibility — Verify your and co-borrower's eligibility, CIBIL score
  4. Submit Application — Apply online or at branch with all required documents
  5. Property Valuation — Bank evaluates collateral (for secured loans)
  6. Loan Sanction — Bank issues sanction letter (typically 2-4 weeks)
  7. Documentation — Sign loan agreement and submit original property docs
  8. Disbursement — Bank transfers funds directly to university (in stages)

Important Timeline Note

Start your loan application 3-4 months before your course start date. Secured loans take 3-6 weeks for processing. Don't wait until the last minute — this is the #1 reason students miss their intake deadlines.

10. Tax Benefits Under Section 80E

The Indian government offers significant tax relief on education loans:

  • What's deductible? — The entire interest paid on the education loan (no cap on amount)
  • Who can claim? — The person who repays the loan (student or parent)
  • Duration — Available for 8 consecutive years from the year you start repaying
  • Applicable to — Loans from scheduled banks and approved financial institutions

Example Tax Saving

If you pay ₹3,00,000 in interest annually and are in the 30% tax bracket, you save ₹90,000 in taxes each year under Section 80E. Over 8 years, this can add up to ₹7-10 Lakhs in total tax savings.

11. Expert Tips to Get Approved

  1. Check your CIBIL score early — Get your and co-borrower's scores 3-4 months before applying. Fix any issues.
  2. Target QS Top 200 universities — Banks offer better rates and higher amounts for top-ranked universities
  3. Maintain clean bank statements — 6 months of clean, consistent transactions. Avoid large unexplained deposits.
  4. Apply to multiple banks — Compare at least 3 offers before committing. Negotiate on processing fees.
  5. Keep documents organized — Missing documents are the #1 reason for loan delays. Use Abbacy's checklist.
  6. Show scholarship/assistantship — Even partial scholarships significantly improve your loan approval chances
  7. Consider hybrid funding — Use savings + scholarship + loan to reduce the total loan amount
  8. Simple interest during study — Opt for this if your family can afford it — saves lakhs over the loan tenure

12. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Waiting until the last moment — Loan processing takes 3-6 weeks. Start early!
  • ❌ Not checking CIBIL score — A score below 650 can result in outright rejection
  • ❌ Applying with incomplete documents — This causes unnecessary delays and multiple bank visits
  • ❌ Over-borrowing — Take only what you need. Factor in scholarships and part-time work income abroad
  • ❌ Ignoring NBFCs for non-collateral — HDFC Credila, Avanse offer great options without property
  • ❌ Not comparing EMI options — Simple interest during study vs. compound interest can save ₹2-5 Lakhs
  • ❌ Forgetting about the moratorium — Use it wisely! Start earning via OPT/internship during this period
  • ❌ Not claiming Section 80E — Many families forget this — you lose lakhs in tax savings
