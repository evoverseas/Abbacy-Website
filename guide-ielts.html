1. Quick Overview

Choosing the right English proficiency test is one of the most important decisions in your study abroad journey. The test you pick impacts your application timeline, university options, and overall success. Here's a quick snapshot:

  • IELTS — The most widely accepted, paper or computer-based, 2h 45min
  • PTE Academic — Fully computer-based, AI-scored, results in 48 hours
  • Duolingo English Test — Cheapest and quickest, at-home on your computer, 1 hour
  • TOEFL iBT — Preferred by US universities, computer-based, 2 hours

3. IELTS — In-Depth Breakdown

Test Structure

SectionDurationFormat
Listening30 minutes4 recordings, 40 questions
Reading60 minutes3 passages, 40 questions
Writing60 minutes2 tasks (150 + 250 words)
Speaking11–14 minutes3-part face-to-face interview

Pros & Cons

  • ✅ Universally accepted — recognized by virtually every university worldwide
  • ✅ Human examiner for speaking — some students perform better in conversation
  • ✅ Paper option available — for those who prefer writing by hand
  • ❌ Slower results — 13 days for paper-based
  • ❌ Writing is hand-scored — can be subjective
  • ❌ Higher test fee — ₹16,250

4. PTE Academic — In-Depth Breakdown

Test Structure

SectionDurationFormat
Speaking & Writing54–67 minutesRead aloud, essay, summarize
Reading29–30 minutesMultiple choice, reorder, fill blanks
Listening30–43 minutesAudio clips, summarize, fill blanks

Pros & Cons

  • ✅ AI-scored — no human bias, consistent scoring
  • ✅ Fast results — typically within 48 hours
  • ✅ Integrated skills — some tasks test multiple skills at once, efficient for strong students
  • ✅ Unlimited score sends — send to as many universities as you want, free
  • ❌ Computer-only — requires fast typing skills
  • ❌ Accent sensitivity — microphone quality and pronunciation matter more
  • ❌ Less accepted for UK immigration — UKVI only accepts specific PTE variants

Expert Tip from Abbacy Global

PTE is often the best choice for students who are comfortable with technology, type fast, and want quick results. Many students who score Band 6 on IELTS score 65+ on PTE with focused preparation. Our trainers can help you decide which test maximizes your score.

  • ✅ Take from home — no test center needed, just laptop + webcam
  • ✅ Results in 48 hours
  • ✅ Accepted by 4,000+ institutions including many top US and UK universities
  • ✅ Retake anytime — no waiting period
  • ❌ Not accepted everywhere — many Australian and some UK universities don't accept it
  • ❌ Newer test — some immigration authorities don't recognize it
  • ❌ Strict proctoring — looking away from screen can flag your test

6. What About TOEFL iBT?

TOEFL is still popular for US universities and some Canadian programs. Key highlights:

  • 2-hour computer-based test
  • Scored 0-120 (30 per section: Reading, Listening, Speaking, Writing)
  • Test fee: $190 (~₹15,800)
  • Most preferred by US universities after GRE
  • Speaking section is recorded (not face-to-face)

8. Which Test Should You Take?

Here's our expert recommendation based on 15+ years of helping students:

  • Going to UK or Canada? → Take IELTS Academic — it's the gold standard
  • Going to Australia? → Take PTE Academic — widely preferred and fast results
  • Going to USA? → TOEFL or IELTS are safest; Duolingo works for many universities
  • On a tight budget? → Duolingo — at $59 it's 1/3rd the cost of others
  • Need results urgently? → PTE or Duolingo — both give results in 48 hours
  • Prefer human interaction? → IELTS — face-to-face speaking test
  • Strong at typing? → PTE — integrated format rewards fast typists

10. Preparation Tips

General Tips (All Tests)

  1. Know the format — Practice with official mock tests before booking
  2. Give yourself 6–8 weeks — Minimum preparation time for a strong score
  3. Focus on weaknesses — Identify your weak sections and allocate more time
  4. Practice daily — 2-3 hours of focused study every day
  5. Take full-length mock tests — At least 4-5 before the real test
  6. Improve vocabulary — Read English articles, watch English videos daily

IELTS-Specific Tips

  • Practice writing Task 1 (graphs/charts) and Task 2 (essays) separately
  • Time yourself strictly — many fail because of time management
  • Record yourself speaking and listen back for pronunciation errors

PTE-Specific Tips

  • Improve your typing speed — aim for 40+ WPM
  • Practice "Read Aloud" extensively — it impacts reading AND speaking scores
  • Use templates for essay and summarize written text tasks

Duolingo-Specific Tips

  • Practice the adaptive format on the free Duolingo practice test
  • Ensure strong internet connection and a quiet, well-lit room
  • Don't look away from the screen — the AI proctoring will flag you
